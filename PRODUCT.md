# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Developers, AI enthusiasts, and researchers running local LLMs on their own hardware. Primary context: sitting at a desktop machine, iterating on prompts and configurations, comparing models and engines to find the optimal setup for their hardware.

- **Power users** who run local models regularly, tweak settings, and compare backends
- **App developers** integrating local LLMs, needing latency profiles for UX decisions
- **Researchers** benchmarking models across quantization levels and engines

## Product Purpose

TokRate makes it trivial to understand, compare, and optimize local LLM inference performance. It connects to any OpenAI-compatible endpoint (Ollama, vLLM, llama.cpp, LM Studio, SGLang), streams responses with live performance metrics, and enables side-by-side comparison of engines, models, and quantizations. Success looks like a polished, shipped desktop tool that the local LLM community relies on.

## Brand Personality

Precise, clean, authoritative. A professional instrument — not a toy. Think browser DevTools or a high-end oscilloscope: dense with information, but organized. Every pixel communicates competence and trustworthiness.

## Anti-references

- Purple/indigo SaaS gradient-aesthetics
- "Friendly" rounded cards with heavy shadows
- Cluttered dashboards with meaningless charts
- Generic admin panel templates
- Over-animated UI that prioritizes flash over function

## Design Principles

1. **Data is the hero** — charts and numbers take center stage. Chrome, not chrome.
2. **Respect the user's attention** — every element earns its place. No decorative flourishes that don't carry information.
3. **Dark mode first** — the user is at a desk, often at night, focused on the output. Light mode is secondary.
4. **Precision in every detail** — alignment, contrast, spacing, typography. If it's worth showing, it's worth showing well.
5. **Progressive disclosure** — surface the essential metrics immediately; tuck advanced controls behind deliberate reveals.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (4.5:1 text contrast)
- Dark and light themes
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Charts must not rely solely on color — patterns or labels included
- Keyboard navigable throughout
